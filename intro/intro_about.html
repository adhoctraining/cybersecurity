LEARNING OUTCOMES

Upon completion of this course the student should have a fundamental understanding of computer and network security to include:
  1. Security vulnerabilities ans risk assessment.
  2. Attack mechanisms and techniques (DDoS, SQIi, etc)
  3. Intrusion detection and prevention
  4. Cryptographic systems
  5. Incidence response
  6. Diaster recovery
  7. Implement security policies, including compliance and operational security.
  8. Implement authentication and access control.
  9. Describe the basic components of computer networking(i.e. firewalls.
  10. Administer computer, group, and user accounts (linux).
  11. Basic knowledge of linux (navigation, creation of users and groups and scripting).
  12. Network fingerprinting (ping sweeps, port scanning, protocol analysis).
  13. Implement secure network designs, security appliances and protocols and system hardening.

COURSE DESCRIPTION

As the title states, this course is an introduction to cybersecurity. What the title dowsn't state is that it is so much more. Mastery of the fundamentals is key to being successful regarding the subject matter. In this course the students body of knowledge is improved by initially introducing key concepts. Additionally, actual cases invovling data breaches and the indictments tht followed are reviewed to further the students knowledge. Finally, labs have been constructed to simulate a data breach. The purpose being to understand the mind of the hacker, but more importantly how to forensically look for indicators of compromise (IOC).

Basic understanding of linux and bash scripting is essential and is covered in this course as small introduction to hacking with bash. The added benefit in learning linux is that many of the penetration tools are of you with An introduction to the fundamentals of cybersecurity and information assurance. Students will develop a knowledge base for defining and recognizing both online threats and potential targets and develop intellectual tools for evaluating relative risks within cyberspace. Students will apply theories and best practices for addressing potential costs of countermeasures for cyber-attacks. Offered fall and spring. Lecture and lab.
